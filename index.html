Internship

During my internship, I worked at Cakrabhuana tower in Cirebon as an Air Traffic Controller that control the traffic by visual. This experience was learn me how to work by team in the tower with guidance from professional ATCs and provided me with an amazing feeling of responsibility and accomplishment. As a tower controller, I was responsible for separating traffic taking off and landing at the airport, and sometimes placing them in holding patterns until they could safely land. This opportunity was a crucial part of my journey to become a competent and professional ATC, and I am grateful for the experience.

The desk control of Cakrabhuana tower in Cirebon plays a vital role in ensuring the safety and efficiency of air traffic within the designated airspace. ATCs assigned to this desk are responsible for monitoring and managing air traffic, as well as directing aircraft on the ground and through a given section of controlled airspace. They also provide advisory services to aircraft in non-controlled airspace and communicate with pilots by radio. Additionally, the control desk enforces traffic separation rules to prevent collisions and ensure each aircraft maintains a minimum amount of empty space around it at all times.

During my second internship at Fatmawati tower in Bengkulu as an Air Traffic Controller, I had the opportunity to control air traffic based on reporting by pilots. This required me to communicate with pilots and provide them with instructions and clearance for takeoff, landing, and other movements on the runway. I had to carefully monitor the movements of aircraft and ensure that they were safely separated from each other. This experience allowed me to develop my skills in communication, decision-making, and situational awareness.
